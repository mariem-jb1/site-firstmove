Educational System in China

Higher education in China is institutions classified according to the courses they offer. The study system in China is quite elitist and access to universities is becoming increasingly selective.

The higher education system in China takes the following form:

  • Benke: bac + 4 which is equivalent to a license from the Moroccan education system;
  • Shuoshi: bac + 5 to bac + 7, which corresponds to the Master;
  • Boshi: bac + 8 to bac + 10, which corresponds to the doctoral cycle.

Why studying in china ?

First of all it opens to many opportunities for international students to view things from Chinese perspectives. Merging the knowledge of both China and their home countries will give students an advantage to explore potential opportunities in China for the future. China has become one of the most popular destinations for international students to study abroad because of it’s long history and fascinating cultures.

Both living costs and tuitions are inexpensive, especially when compared to that from the United States and many other western countries. International students can both study and evensave money while study in China. There are scholarships from the government, universities, foundations and corporations designed to support international students from different academic disciplines.

International students can receive education with medium of instruction in English. Program taught in English is a convenient option for international students who want to pursue a degree program in China, but don’t want to spend two extra years in Chinese language program.

scholarships

No matter which major you study at a foreign university, finance will always be an important factor. It’s highly recommended that you understand the costs of studying abroad, which generally include tuition fees, accommodation, travel, living expenses and so on. Now there are 5 kinds of ISSP scholarships available to international students, which are:

  • Type A

Free Tuition & Accommodation Fee with Stipend in Entire Duration.


Type A scholarships are mostly sponsored by the Chinese government, which have the highest requirements and take much longer to get the result as well.

  • Type B

Free Tuition & Accommodation Fee in Entire Duration Without Stipend


  • Type C

Free tuition Fee in Entire Duration.


Type B and Type C scholarships are usually sponsored by local government and schools, which are more likely to offer scholarships for those students who have outstanding academic scores.

  • Type D

Partial Scholarship in Entire Duration.


  • Type E

Free Tuition Fee in 1st Year.


Type D and Type E scholarships are usually sponsored by enterprises. A lot of universities who joined ISSP Scholarship Program created by ScholarshipChina offer Type D and Type E scholarships, which tend to be easier to obtain.
